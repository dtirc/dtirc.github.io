Multi-scale modeling for digital twin shop-floor


A multi-layer modeling framework for supporting model construction from unit layer to system layer to system of system layer was proposed firstly. Moreover, the mechanism of model changes over time is also considered. Then, this work gave the specific procedures and methods of model assembly, model fusion and model update towards machines and shop-floor respectively. Finally, a satellite AIT(Assembly, Integration and Test) shop-floor is chosen as the case to validate the correctness and feasibility of proposed framework, procedures and methods.

Digital twin modeling (DTM)


Digital twin, as an effective means to realize the fusion between physical and virtual spaces, has attracted more and more attention in the past few years. Based on ultra-fidelity models, more accurate service, e.g., real-time monitoring and failure prediction, can be carried out. Our research aims to provide guidelines for developing complex digital twin models, evaluating them, and updating them to maintain consistency between the virtual and physical spaces.

Connection and Interaction towards Digital Twin


Connection and interaction between the physical and virtual spaces is considered an essential aspect to deploy digital twin. An ideal condition for implementing digital twin is the realization of physical-virtual consistency, where virtual models enable to accurately, constantly reflect the physical scenarios in real-time. Our research aims to develop a theoretical system of digital twin connection and interaction, and discuss a series of revelant methods focusing on the establishment of data-model coupling relations, model consistency and digital twin-based solutions.

Consistency evaluation for digital twin models


A consistency evaluation framework towards digital twin shop-floor (DTS) models was developed. The consistency evaluation method mainly contains two phases, i.e., before and after model assembly and model fusion. Before model assembly and model fusion, the geometry models, physics models, behavior models, and rule models are mainly considered. After model assembly and fusion, the overall performance of assembled models and fusion models is paid more attention. Furthermore, the analytic hierarchy process (AHP) was used to form a comprehensive consistency evaluation result for DTS models. At last, a specific application scenario in complex product AIT (Assembly, Integration and Test) shop-floor was given to explain how to apply the proposed framework and method.

  • Zhang, H., Qi, Q., & Tao, F. (2022) A consistency evaluation method for digital twin models. Journal of Manufacturing Systems, 65, 158-168.

Model update for digital twin multi-dimension models


A general digital twin model update framework was developed. Then, the update methods for multi-dimension models are further explored. In this work, a cutting tool was taken as the application object to discuss how to conduct physics model update based on the proposed framework and methods. Through model update, a more accurate and updated tool wear model could be obtained, which contributes to the prognostics and health management for machine tools.

  • Zhang, H., Qi, Q., Ji, W., & Fei, T. (2023) An update method for digital twin multi-dimension models. Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing, 80, 102481.

Digital twin equipment


To further improve intelligence and performance of equipment, the concept of digital twin equipment was first proposed from four aspects, including definition, composition, ideal capabilities and key technologies. Then, development stages of digital twin equipment were further discussed. Finally, the application of the proposed digital twin equipment was verified by logistics equipment in textile shop-floor and autoclave in composite processing shop-floor.

  • Tao, F., Zhang, C., Zhang, H., et al. (2022). Future equipment exploration: digital twin equipment. Computer Integrated Manufacturing Systems, 28(1), 1-16 (in Chinese).

Digital twin maturity model


The digital twin maturity model was put forward to help correctly understand, evaluate and enhance digital twin applications. Firstly, six levels of digital twin maturity were established. Then 19 digital twin maturity evaluation factors were proposed for operational digital twin maturity evaluation. Finally, the application process of digital twin maturity model was illustrated in detail with two application examples toward unit-level digital twin and system-level digital twin respectively.

  • Tao, F., Zhang, C., Qi, Q., et al. (2022). Digital twin maturity model. Computer Integrated Manufacturing Systems, 2022, 28(5), 1267-1281 (in Chinese).
