Data-model fusion


Over the past few years, data-driven methods have demonstrated their efficacy and potency across various industrial applications. Nonetheless, they suffer from issues related to data dependency and a lack of interpretability. To mitigate these challenges associated with the pure data-driven approaches, our research aims to develop hybrid data model fusion approaches to combine model-based methods with data-driven methods to reduce data dependency and enhance the interpretability of pure data-driven approaches.

Related works

Prediction of bearing degradation


A hybrid simulation model and data-driven approach were developed for bearing degradation stage classification and remaining useful life predictions. This work leveraged a digital twin-enabled model for the generation of simulated vibration signals, which were then used to train a similarity-based data-driven method. This hybrid approach enhanced the precision of both degradation stage classification and remaining useful life predictions for bearings.

Life prediction of lithium-ion batteries


A physics-informed data-driven approach was developed for battery state-of-health and remaining useful life predictions. In this work, a physical battery aging model was integrated with a transformer-based data-driven method to incorporate various aging factors of lithium-ion batteries into data-driven methods. This integration allows for a more accurate estimation of the degradation trajectory of aging batteries, improving the accuracy of state-of-health and remaining useful life predictions.

Real-time quality monitoring of additive manufacturing process


A hybrid process parameters model and data-driven approach was developed for real-time quality monitoring in the additive manufacturing process. In this work, a process parameter model was integrated with a graph convolutional network-based data-driven method to consider the impact of both process parameters and printing variations on the quality of additively manufactured parts. This integration enables a more effective real-time quality monitoring during the additive manufacturing process.
