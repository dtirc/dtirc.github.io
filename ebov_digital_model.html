Mechanism-driven modeling on EBOV protein subcellular assembly


We used ODE-based modeling approach to simulate the replication and assembly process of EBOV. New mechanisms in the assembly process of VP40 and NP are found and proposed in our work. The model is also used for conducting in silico trial of fendiline, a potential treatment for EBOV infection on subcellular level. The model could be part of the multiscale modeling to simulate the whole infection process of EBOV in human.

  • X. Liu, E. Pappas, M. Husby et al. "Mechanisms of phosphatidylserine influence on viral production: a computational model of Ebola virus matrix protein assembly". In: Journal of Biological Chemistry 298 (2022) 7. doi: 10.1016/j.jbc.2022.102025

EBOV digital model


Ebolavirus (EBOV) is a deadly pathogen with around 90% mortality rate. Research on EBOV is limited to BSL-4 labs, which hinders the process. We hope to assist the study and treatment development of EBOV by computational modeling simulation of its subcellular replication and assembly process. We ultimately hope to create “virtual EBOV” which could replace the real virus for research and treatment evaluation.
