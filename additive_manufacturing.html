Energy modeling and prediction for powder bed fusion and material extrusion


In order to enable the prediction and evaluation of the energy efficiency of additive manufacturing processes in the design stage, a function-oriented modeling method for mapping the power flow in AM systems and a G-Code-driven energy simulation approach have been proposed. The proposed methods are further applied to a powder bed fusion (PBF) and a material extrusion (ME) additive manufacturing machines. Finally, the energy assessment is integrated into a framework of eco-design for AM.

4D printing


This research focuses on thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU)-based shape memory polymer (SMP) and systematically investigates its performance using experiments. In the experiments, the printing quality of the SMP material under difference process parameter combination has been analyzed and the shape-changing process in difference ambient temperature are evaluated. After that, use cases of different product design are carried out to demonstrate the potentials of 4D printing, in which the shape-changing functional products are produced to fulfill difference use demands.

Digital twin for process monitoring of material extrusion


This study aims at the machine level and adopts augmented reality (AR) to develop a digital twin for a material extrusion printer. To provide a digital representation of the printing process of a component, this study has proposed an approach using cumulated small cylinders to approximate the geometry of the component, which is called “Volume Approximation by Cumulated Cylinders (VACCY)”. To monitor the economic and environmental performance, this study has modeled four process indicators (electricity use, manufacturing cost, greenhouse gas emission, and primary energy consumption) and integrated them in the AR-based digital twin. Through the test of the developed digital twin for printing three components, the feasibility and performance of the developed digital twin are well demonstrated

  • L. Yi, M. Glatt, S. Ehmsen, W. Duan, and J. Aurich. “Process monitoring of economic and environmental performance of a material extrusion printer using an augmented reality-based digital twin”. In: Additive Manufacturing 48 (2021).
    doi: 10.1016/j.addma.2021.102388.

Additive manufacturing


Additive manufacturing (AM) enables the design and production of complex products that are difficult or impossible to manufacture with conventional manufacturing processes. Addressing design benefits of AM requires comprehensive research efforts to enhance product quality and process performance. The focus at DTIRC spans product design, process optimization, sustainability assessment, and 4D printing, aiming to uncover economic and environmental advantages across various AM processes, in order to propel AM into a realm of sustainability, efficiency, and innovation.

Potentials of AM for commercial vehicle production


AM technologies show numerous potentials in terms of rapid prototyping, tooling and direct manufacturing of functional parts and implies revolutionary benefits for the manufacturing industry. Currently, many industrial areas are marching to a more comprehensive application of AM. Hence, the development of new tools, methods, and concepts for guiding companies to implement AM technologies requires more research attention. This work introduces the results of a research project carried out by academic and industrial partners from the commercial vehicle industry. The research project addressed the issues for a long-term application of AM technologies, such topology optimization for vehicle components, identification of barriers for impeding the AM applications, cost estimation for AM application, design of hybrid additive-subtractive process chains, and quality management with AM.

SPM in a specified period for FDM


The Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) process that builds parts layer-by-layer is accomplished in a specified manufacturing period (number of layers) without interruption or suspension. Thus, traditional SPM methods, where the average run length is used for the calculation of the control limits and the measurement of the performance, are no longer applicable to the FDM process. an SPM method is proposed based on the surface image data of FDM parts with a specified period.

  • Huang, T., Wang S., Yang S., & Dai W. (2021). Statistical Process Monitoring in a Specified Period for the Image Data of Fused Deposition Modeling Parts with Consistent Layers. Journal of Intelligent Manufacturing 32 (8): 2181–96. https://doi.org/10.1007/s10845-020-01628-4.
  • Li, Q., Huang, T., & Wang S. (2023). Statistical Process Monitoring Based on Region Growing for Fused Deposition Modeling with Image Data. 2023 Global Reliability and Prognostics and Health Management (PHM-Hangzhou).
