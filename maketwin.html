Research fields/makeTwin

The makeTwin software architecture


The fundamental theory and critical common technologies of digital twins are progressively finding deeper applications, underscoring the pressing need for a versatile research and development platform in this domain. In response to the dynamic demands of the digital twin market, our objective is to create the makeTwin software architecture—a comprehensive, open, and compatible solution—to propel the practical applications of digital twins in engineering.

Related works

makeTwin：A reference architecture for digital twin software platform


The passage analyzes the seven major demands of diverse users for digital twin industrial software, and proposes and designs a reference architecture for a digital twin software platform called makeTwin. It outlines the ten core functions of digital twin industrial software, as well as the basic workflow and interactive mechanisms of these core functions.

White Paper: Comprehensive guide on digital twin industrial software


This book offers an in-depth analysis of the current global and domestic landscape of digital twins and digital twin industrial software development. It defines digital twin industrial software and elucidates industry demands from diverse viewpoints. Following the framework of the five-dimensional model of digital twins, the book systematically reviews the foundational research conducted by scholars worldwide in the theoretical and technical aspects of digital twin industrial software. Drawing on the groundwork laid in earlier research, it presents the reference architecture for makeTwin digital twin industrial software. In conclusion, the book provides an overview of the current state of the digital twin software platform in China.

  • Tao F, Sun XM, Cheng JF, et al. makeTwin: A reference architecture for digital twin software platform. In: Chinese Journal of Aeronautics, (2023).
  • Tao F, Qi Q. Make more digital twins. In: Nature, (2019) 573 (7775): 490-491.

Patent: makeTwin - A digital twin industrial software architecture


This patent introduces six functional modules of makeTwin. The TwinServiceTemplate module facilitates the provision of adaptable service templates, ensuring flexibility. TwinAlgSolver specializes in algorithmic problem-solving, enhancing the software's analytical prowess. With TwinModelCreator, intricate twin models are generated seamlessly, while TwinDataProcessor efficiently manages and processes twin-related data. The TwinInteractor module enables seamless configurations for virtual-real interaction, enhancing user engagement. Lastly, the TwinConnector module establishes robust communication connections, ensuring smooth data exchange. Together, these modules define the powerful foundation of the makeTwin architecture, poised to redefine the landscape of digital twin industrial software.

Production Logistics Digital Twins (PLDTs) System


A chemical fiber shopfloor digital twin system was developed for digital operation and maintenance of shopfloor production logistics. In this work, the human-computer interaction interface development, high-fidelity digital twin model construction for chemical fiber shopfloor, 3D visualization and monitoring, shopfloor operation strategy optimization, and fault diagnosis and prediction of production logistics equipment were investigated respectively.

  • Tao F, Sun XM, Cheng JF, et al. makeTwin: A reference architecture for digital twin software platform. In: Chinese Journal of Aeronautics, (2023).
  • Zhu Y, Cheng J, Liu Z, et al. Production logistics digital twins: Research profiling, application, challenges and opportunities[J]. Robotics and Computer-Integrated Manufacturing, 2023, 84: 102592.

The research profiling of PLDTs was analyzed based on general trends, keywords, application scenarios and basic functions. Secondly, the application advantages, limitations and key supporting technologies of PLDTs in application scenarios such as transportation, packaging, warehousing, material distribution and information processing were summarized from the perspective of functional characteristics. Furthermore, the possible challenges and future directions of PLDTs in industrial applications were presented with an appropriate classification and extensive recommendations.
